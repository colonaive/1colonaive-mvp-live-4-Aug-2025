# ColonAiQ Scientific and Regulatory Overview

## Technology Summary

ColonAiQ is a multiplex ctDNA methylation PCR assay designed for colorectal cancer (CRC) screening using plasma samples obtained from peripheral blood.

The assay detects abnormal DNA methylation signatures associated with colorectal tumorigenesis.

Detection workflow:

1. Whole blood collection
2. Plasma separation
3. cfDNA extraction
4. Bisulfite conversion
5. Target pre-amplification
6. qPCR methylation detection
7. Result interpretation based on Ct thresholds

The assay identifies circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) methylation markers associated with colorectal cancer.

---

# Current Singapore HSA Registered Version

The currently registered ColonAiQ configuration in Singapore HSA uses a **5-marker methylation panel**.

Markers included:

• SEPTIN9  
• IKZF1  
• BCAT1  
• VAV3  
• SEPTIN9-2

These markers are detected through multiplex qPCR reactions after bisulfite conversion of circulating DNA.

The test is intended as a **screening and risk stratification tool**, not a definitive diagnostic assay.

Important clinical limitation:

Positive results must be confirmed with **colonoscopy and histopathology**.

Negative results **do not rule out colorectal cancer**.

---

# Upcoming 6-Marker Version (Regulatory Alignment)

The upcoming ColonAiQ configuration expands the biomarker panel to **6 methylation markers**.

Markers:

• SEPTIN9  
• SEPTIN9 region 2  
• BCAT1  
• IKZF1  
• BCAN  
• VAV3  

This expanded marker panel is used in the **CE IVDR version** of the assay and will be aligned with:

• EU CE IVDR registration  
• Singapore HSA update  
• India CDSCO submission  

The purpose of expanding to six markers is to improve detection sensitivity through **multi-locus methylation signatures**, which capture a broader representation of tumor epigenetic changes.

---

# Regulatory Strategy

The regulatory strategy is to **align the final commercial configuration across major regulatory jurisdictions**.

Target alignment:

EU CE IVDR – Class C IVD  
Singapore HSA – Class C Medical Device  
India CDSCO – Class C IVD  

Aligning the marker configuration across these jurisdictions reduces regulatory fragmentation and simplifies future global expansion.

Benefits of alignment:

• single technical dossier structure  
• consistent clinical evidence package  
• simplified post-market surveillance  
• easier ASEAN expansion

---

# Performance Characteristics

Published clinical validation studies demonstrate:

Sensitivity for CRC detection: ~86–89%  
Specificity: ~86–92% depending on population cohort.

Multi-locus methylation detection provides improved sensitivity compared with single-marker blood tests.

---

# Clinical Role

ColonAiQ is intended for:

• colorectal cancer screening  
• triage for colonoscopy  
• potential recurrence monitoring in future applications

It is positioned as a **non-invasive blood-based screening test** that improves patient compliance compared with stool-based screening tests and invasive colonoscopy.
