# CRC News Intelligence — Editorial Rules

## Purpose

Define the editorial framework for translating colorectal cancer research news into ethical public education and marketing content for COLONAiVE and ColonAiQ.

## Core Principles

### 1. Scientific Accuracy First

- All claims must be traceable to published, peer-reviewed research
- Quote sensitivity/specificity figures only from the specific study referenced
- Never extrapolate findings beyond the study population or methodology
- Label preliminary or pre-print findings as such

### 2. IFU Compliance (Non-Negotiable)

- A positive ColonAiQ result does **not** equal a cancer diagnosis
- A negative ColonAiQ result does **not** rule out colorectal cancer
- All results are for clinician reference only
- ColonAiQ is positioned as a screening complement to colonoscopy, not a replacement

### 3. No Direct Promotional Language

- Research summaries must educate, not sell
- Avoid phrases like "best test", "most accurate", "breakthrough detection"
- Position screening as a public health benefit, not a product feature
- Never compare ColonAiQ favorably to competitors in patient-facing content

### 4. Patient Empowerment Over Fear

- Use empowering language: "early detection saves lives", "screening gives you options"
- Avoid fear-based messaging: do not lead with mortality statistics
- Frame screening as an act of self-care and responsibility
- Always encourage consultation with a healthcare professional

### 5. Screening Advocacy

- Every piece of content should reinforce the importance of regular CRC screening
- Highlight the 2-step model: non-invasive blood test followed by colonoscopy for positives
- Reference Singapore's national screening guidelines where applicable
- Support the COLONAiVE mission of increasing screening uptake

## Content Categories

### Research Breakthroughs
- New biomarker discoveries
- Large-scale clinical trial results
- Guideline updates from NCCN, ASCO, or equivalent bodies

### Screening Uptake Data
- Population-level screening statistics
- Corporate wellness program outcomes
- Government policy changes affecting screening

### Technology Developments
- Advances in liquid biopsy and methylation-based detection
- Multi-cancer early detection (MCED) developments
- Cost-effectiveness studies

### Patient Stories & Outcomes
- Early detection success stories (with consent)
- Survivorship data supporting screening value
- Community engagement outcomes

| Channel | Tone | Audience | Frequency |
|---------|------|----------|-----------|
| LinkedIn | Professional, evidence-based | HCPs, corporate wellness, investors | 2–3x/week |
| Clinic bulletin | Clinical, practical | GPs, specialists, lab partners | Monthly |
| Patient education | Plain language, empowering | General public, screening candidates | As needed |
| Website news feed | Informative, accessible | All visitors | Continuous |

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing any CRC intelligence content:

- [ ] Source attributed and verifiable
- [ ] No diagnostic claims about ColonAiQ
- [ ] IFU guardrails respected
- [ ] No direct promotional language in patient-facing content
- [ ] Colonoscopy follow-up message included where relevant
- [ ] Language reviewed for accessibility (aim for 8th-grade reading level)
- [ ] Market-specific regulatory context accurate (Singapore, India, Philippines)
